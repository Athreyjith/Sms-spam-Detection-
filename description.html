Spam Awareness

What is Spam?

Spam refers to unsolicited messages sent in bulk over electronic messaging systems, typically advertising or fraudulent in nature.
It often includes unwanted emails, texts, or calls that can be annoying or harmful to recipients.
Spam messages can attempt to steal personal information, distribute malware, or deceive recipients into financial scams.
Effective spam filters and awareness of common tactics are essential for protecting against spam.
Reporting and avoiding interaction with spam messages helps reduce its spread and impact.

Types of Spam

Phishing

Phishing spam aims to trick recipients into revealing sensitive information by posing as a trustworthy entity.

Malware

Malware spam contains attachments or links that, when clicked, install malicious software on the recipient's device.

Advertising

Advertising spam promotes products or services, often unsolicited, and may include deceptive claims or offers.

Fraudulent Offers

Fraudulent offers spam attempts to deceive recipients with false promises of money, prizes, or opportunities.

Chain Letters

Chain letters urge recipients to forward messages to others, often falsely claiming good luck or dire consequences.

Guidelines for Handling Spam

  1. Avoid opening emails or messages from unknown senders.
  2. Do not click on links or download attachments in suspicious messages.
  3. Use spam filters and antivirus software to detect and block spam.
  4. Report spam messages to your email or messaging provider.
  5. Do not disclose personal information in response to unsolicited messages.
  6. Stay informed about common spam tactics and updates in security measures.
  7. Be cautious of emails or messages claiming urgent action is required.
  8. Verify the legitimacy of offers or requests before responding.
  9. Check email headers for inconsistencies or suspicious details.
  10. Use separate email addresses for different purposes (personal, work) to manage spam
  11. Educate yourself and others about recognizing and avoiding spam.
  12. Regularly update passwords and account security settings.
